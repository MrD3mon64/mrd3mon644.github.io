SPEEDBOTS

The SpeedBot is designed to promote road safety through technology. Equipped with an advanced ultrasonic sensor, the vehicle can detect any object 20 cm away. Once it identifies an obstacle, the car immediately stops, preventing potential collisions. In addition to its automatic braking capability, the SpeedBot uses an intelligent decision-making system: after stopping, it evaluates the space around it, turning towards the side that is free of obstructions. This ability is ideal for dense traffic environments, providing greater safety in driving.

The SpeedBot also includes remote control functionality via a mobile app that we developed. With the app, the user can precisely maneuver the car, making it a useful tool both for safe driving practice and for exploring its technology in controlled spaces. Its design combines a robust chassis and a servo motor that ensures efficient and smooth turns. This car is a practical and educational tool that, besides its functionality in road safety, offers an interactive experience for those wishing to learn more about automated driving technologies.

MATERIALS

CHASSIS

Sturdy plastic chassis to ensure lightness and durability. I used additional support structures to securely fasten the motors and other components.

WHEELS

Robust wheels suitable for varied terrains, ensuring the axles were strong enough to support the weight of the vehicle and sudden movements.

BLUETOOTH

Bluetooth module for remote control, ensuring stable and efficient communication within the desired operating area.

ARDUINO

Arduino Uno simplifies setup and programming. This Arduino controls both the vehicle's movement and the automatic detection system.

H-BRIDGE

It reverses the current polarity reaching the motor, allowing the motor to change direction. This is essential for a remote-controlled car, as it enables the vehicle to move forward or backward as needed.

MOTORS

DC motors for propulsion, implementing an H-bridge to control the direction of rotation. This allows them to move forward and backward using the L293D chip.

SERVOMOTOR

Receives information from the pins, allowing it to command the wheels to move in the required direction and speed.

ULTRASONIC SENSOR

PIR sensor for automatic obstacle detection and other vehicles. We adjusted the sensitivity parameters for fast and accurate responses during testing.

BATTERIES

Rechargeable batteries to power the Arduino and motors, correctly calculating battery capacity to maintain the desired operational duration.
